Who is Mary Anning?

childhood disaster

Mary Anning was born on 21 May 1799. She lived in the English seaside own of Lyme Regis in Dorset. When she was 8 months old, her mum and two other women were holding her outside, but then thunderstruck and they rushed under a tree to protect the baby. They were fine until lightning hit the tree, leaving the three women dead but Mary alive. People thought the lightning changed her personality, as before it happened she was dull but after it happened she was bright and bubbly.

about mary

Due to the fact Mary's family was poor, she went out with her father and her brother to go find things she could sell on the beach. Mary was only really interested in finding 'curiosities' -fossils- to sell. One day her father was climbing to find fossils. Though he survived, he suffered many injuries.
When she was just 12, she found a massive piece poking out from a cliff. They thought it might be a crocodile, but what she had discovered was an ancient reptile called an ichthyosaur (which means ‘fish lizard’). Mary went on to make more incredible discoveries in her life, including a long-necked marine reptile, called a plesiosaur and a flying reptile called a Dimorphous.

more info

Mary,later on discovered that her job was called paleontologist.

Back when people didn't know about fossils they named some of the ladies' fingers, snake stone, and a devil's toenail.

When she was 12 years old, she made her first discovery. She was on the beach when suddenly, she found a big piece of slate. She knew she had to find out what was in it so she followed the chipping methods her father showed her.

Did you know the process of chipping is called excavating? Unfortunately, Mary didn't get the recognition she wanted as rich london scientist would never feature her in their books.

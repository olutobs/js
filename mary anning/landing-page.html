Who is Mary Anning?

childhood disaster

Mary Anning was born on 21 May 1799. She lived in the English seaside own of Lyme Regis in Dorset. When she was 8 months old, her mum and two other women were holding her outside, but then thunderstruck and they rushed under a tree to protect the baby. They were fine until a lightning strike hit the tree, leaving the three women dead but Mary alive. People thought the lightning strike changed her personality, as before it happened she was dull but after it happened she was bright and bubbly.

about mary

Due to the fact Mary's family was poor, she went out with her father and her brother to go find things she could sell on the beach. Mary was only really interested in finding 'curiosities' -basically fossils- to sell. One day her father was climbing a rock to find a fossils .though he survived he suffered many injuries and to from a result. When she was just 12, they discovered the skull of a mysterious creature poking out from a cliff. They thought it might be a crocodile, but what she had discovered was actually an ancient reptile called an ichthyosaur (which means ‘fish lizard’). Mary went on to make more incredible discoveries in her life, including a long-necked marine reptile called a plesiosaur and a flying reptile called a Dimorphous.

Mary later on discovered that her job was called paleontologist.

Back when people didn't really know about fossils they named some of them ladies' finger, snake stone and a devil's toenail.

When she was 12 year old, she made her first discovery. She was on the beach when suddenly, she found a big piece of slate. She knew she had to find out what was in it so she followed the chipping methods her father showed her.

Did you know the process of chipping is called excavating. Unfortunately, Mary didn't get the recognition she wanted as rich london scientist would never feature her in their books.
